## 1. Purpose of Runtime Authorization Blocking Closure Refinement

Define governance-safe closure refinement criteria for unresolved runtime authorization blockers identified in `P2-GATE-003`.

This refinement preserves governance authorization discipline, prevents ambiguous closure interpretation, and enforces fail-closed closure posture before any future runtime authorization consideration.

## 2. Governance Blocking Closure Principles

- explicit blocker closure requirements: each blocker requires explicit closure criteria and explicit review outcome
- explicit verification requirements: closure decisions must be evidence-backed and review-auditable
- no inferred closure acceptance: partial, assumed, or implied closure is not acceptable
- fail-closed unresolved-condition handling: unresolved or unverifiable closure conditions remain blocking
- explicit review accountability: closure status must remain attributable and governance-review visible

## 3. Ordering Ambiguity Closure Criteria

- explicit ordering precedence reviewability: precedence assumptions must be explicit, inspectable, and review-verifiable
- replay ambiguity closure expectations: replay interpretation ambiguity must be explicitly resolved or remain blocked
- orphan ordering-chain rejection expectations: orphan ordering chains must remain fail-closed unless explicit closure evidence exists
- no hidden coordination assumptions: hidden ordering coordination or implicit synchronization precedence is prohibited

## 4. Reconciliation Ambiguity Closure Criteria

- explicit reconciliation evidence completeness expectations: reconciliation closure requires explicit evidence completeness criteria
- orphan evidence rejection expectations: orphan reconciliation evidence remains fail-closed until explicit closure criteria are satisfied
- lineage continuity verification completeness expectations: lineage continuity verification requirements must be complete and review-verifiable
- no implicit reconciliation repair acceptance: reconciliation closure does not authorize implicit repair or hidden continuity assumptions

## 5. Trace Evidence Completeness Criteria

- explicit evidence continuity expectations: evidence continuity criteria must be declared, reviewable, and consistently applied
- explicit evidence linkage expectations: linkage criteria between authority, lineage, trust, and trace references must be explicit
- stale/incomplete evidence fail-closed posture: stale or incomplete trace evidence remains blocking
- no hidden evidence reconstruction assumptions: hidden reconstruction or inference-based evidence completion is prohibited

## 6. Transport Assumption Closure Constraints

- hidden transport assumption rejection posture: hidden transport assumptions remain blocking conditions
- no implicit synchronization trust: implicit synchronization trust cannot be used as closure evidence
- no implicit session continuity trust: continuity assumptions cannot substitute for governance verification
- governance-subordinate transport posture preservation: transport assumptions remain subordinate to governance constraints at all times

## 7. Quarantine & Recovery Closure Criteria

- explicit quarantine escalation closure expectations: escalation closure requires explicit category-level closure visibility
- explicit recovery verification completeness expectations: recovery closure requires complete, explicit verification criteria
- no implicit recovery continuity acceptance: continuity-based recovery acceptance remains prohibited
- mandatory revalidation visibility: quarantine release closure requires explicit and review-visible revalidation evidence

## 8. Blocking Review Constraints

## 9. Risks & Planning Concerns
