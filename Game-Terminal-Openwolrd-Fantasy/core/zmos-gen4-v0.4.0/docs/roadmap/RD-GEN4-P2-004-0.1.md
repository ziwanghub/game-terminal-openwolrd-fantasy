## 1. Purpose of Transport Abstraction Constraints

Define governance-safe transport abstraction constraints for Gen4 federation planning.

This model exists to prevent hidden transport assumptions, constrain future runtime direction, preserve governance-first boundaries, preserve fail-closed federation posture, and prevent premature runtime/network leakage.

## 2. Governance-First Transport Principles

- transport is subordinate to governance and cannot override governance decisions
- no implicit trust through connectivity is permitted
- no hidden authority propagation through transport-side assumptions is permitted
- ambiguity in transport context must fail-closed
- deterministic governance precedence must remain primary over transport interpretations

## 3. Transport Abstraction Boundary Model

- transport abstraction in this phase is planning-only
- no runtime transport selection is defined or authorized
- no protocol binding is defined or authorized
- no executable session semantics are defined or authorized
- no implicit federation connectivity assumptions are permitted

## 4. Transport Surface Classification

Planning-only categories:

- identity exchange boundary
- governance evidence transfer boundary
- trace correlation boundary
- quarantine/isolation signaling boundary
- recovery/revalidation signaling boundary

Forbidden runtime categories:

- executable transport channels
- runtime session negotiation
- automatic peer discovery
- hidden synchronization
- runtime topology propagation

## 5. Trust & Verification Constraints

- transport cannot establish trust by itself
- governance trust requires explicit authority/trust validation
- unverifiable transport context must fail-closed
- stale, replayed, or unlinked evidence cannot establish active governance posture

## 6. Ordering & Replay Constraints

- transport ordering assumptions cannot override governance ordering rules
- replay ambiguity must fail-closed
- implicit delivery-order trust is forbidden
- deterministic interpretation must remain inspectable

## 7. Isolation & Quarantine Constraints

- transport isolation cannot bypass governance quarantine
- quarantined participants cannot regain active governance posture implicitly
- recovery requires explicit governance revalidation
- isolation violations must fail-closed

## 8. Cross-Boundary Constraints

- no hidden cross-boundary state propagation
- no implicit authority inheritance across transport boundaries
- no hidden transport-side mutation semantics
- no hidden synchronization channels

## 9. Transport Planning Risks

- hidden transport assumption risk may undermine governance-first boundary discipline
- abstraction leakage risk may prematurely bias implementation decisions
- ordering/replay interpretation drift risk may weaken deterministic governance interpretation
- isolation boundary erosion risk may weaken quarantine and fail-closed posture
- cross-boundary mutation ambiguity risk may introduce implicit authority channels

## 10. Planning Review Constraints

- this model is planning-only
- this model is non-executable
- no runtime networking authorization is granted
- no protocol/runtime implementation approval is granted

## 11. Acceptance Criteria

This model is acceptable when:

- transport abstraction boundaries are explicit and governance-subordinate
- trust, ordering, replay, isolation, and cross-boundary constraints are fail-closed aligned
- forbidden runtime categories remain unintroduced
- document language remains non-executable and free from runtime implementation detail
- no transport/runtime leakage is present

## 12. Authorization Statement

This document defines transport abstraction constraints only and does not authorize networking or federation runtime implementation.
